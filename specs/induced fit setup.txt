1. Project Goal

High-fidelity molecular docking of Benzodiazepine (BZD) ligands into α1 (Bound/Unbound) and α2 (Natural State/Apo) GABA-A receptor subtypes using a rigid-receptor engine (GNINA) enhanced by a deep-learning induced-fit stage (DiffDock/DynamicBind).
2. Protein Preparation (Experimental vs. Model)

The "math" of adding hydrogens must respect the structural state of the imaging:

    Holo-Structures (α1 Bound): Keep the ligand in the pocket during protonation. Omit --nodebump. This allows side chains to relax and "hug" the ligand, creating an "Open" pocket. Remove the ligand after protonation.

    Apo-Structures (α2 Natural State): Since the pocket is empty, Use --nodebump. This locks the heavy atoms (C, N, O) in their experimental coordinates and prevents the pocket from collapsing into the vacuum during hydrogen addition.

3. The "Induced Fit" Hybrid Pipeline

To solve the issue of rigid docking into a potentially "tight" natural-state α2, use a two-stage process:
Stage 1: Pose Generation (Flexible)

    Tool: DiffDock or DynamicBind.

    Input: PDB (from PDB2PQR) and SDF (from RDKit/OpenBabel).

    Action: These models perform "Deep Learning Induced Fit," moving the protein side chains to accommodate the BZD scaffold.

    Output: A new PDB of the "Opened" receptor and the docked ligand pose.

Stage 2: High-Fidelity Scoring (Rigid)

    Tool: GNINA.

    Action: Rescore the DiffDock output using GNINA's CNN scoring function.

    Benefit: Combines the flexibility of deep learning with the rigorous affinity prediction of a CNN-based docking engine.
