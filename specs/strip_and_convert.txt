By default, when obabel adds hydrogens (-p 7.4) or calculates partial charges, it often triggers a "helpful" normalization that re-centers the coordinates to the geometric origin. Since you already moved your receptor to 0,0,0 based on the ligand, OpenBabel's re-centering is shifting the protein away from your intended docking site.

Here is the 3-step fix to lock the model space and stop the clashing.
1. Fix the obabel Command

Modify your strip_and_convert.py to include the -xc (Do Not Center) and -xn (Do Not Normalize) flags. This forces OpenBabel to treat your input coordinates as gospel.

Update this block in your script:
Python

    # -xc = Do NOT center the molecule on the origin
    # -xn = Do NOT normalize (keeps your exact atom order/coords)
    obabel_cmd = [
        "obabel", 
        "-ipdb", stripped_pdb, 
        "-opdbqt", 
        "-O", final_pdbqt, 
        "-p", "7.4", 
        "--partialcharge", "gasteiger",
        "-xc", "-xn" 
    ]
